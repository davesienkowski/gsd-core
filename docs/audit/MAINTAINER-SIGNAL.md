# Maintainer Signal — "Cleaner and Tighter"

**Recorded:** 2026-06-07
**Type:** Dated **pre-reconciliation observation** (not a post-hoc rationalization)
**Decision:** D-14

## The observation

> **"Cleaner and tighter."**

Two maintainers independently surfaced this phrase as the desired direction for the
newcomer-readiness work, dated **2026-06-07**. It is recorded **now**, at the start of the
fast-track, deliberately **before** any analysis is reconciled — so it reads as an
**input observation that frames scope**, not as a conclusion retro-fitted to justify findings
the audit later produced.

## Why this is recorded as an observation, not a rationalization

The fast-track is firewalled from prior artifacts (see `README.md`). Recording the maintainer
signal at the front of Milestone 1, dated and attributed, means:

- It is a **known starting constraint**, transparent to anyone reviewing the audit.
- Later quick-win findings can be checked *against* it ("does this make the newcomer path
  cleaner and tighter?") rather than the phrase being invented after the fact to fit them.
- It is auditable: the date and the two-maintainer convergence are on record before the
  streams run.

## How it frames fast-track scope

"Cleaner and tighter" is the **north-star** for Milestone 1 and shapes the three streams:

- **UX / Onboarding (Phase 2):** fewer friction points, clearer first-run signposting — but
  via progressive disclosure, not deletion; safety commands stay.
- **Token-Savings (Phase 3):** "tighter" = less recurring-context tax — but only mechanical
  bloat is cut freely; load-bearing instructional density is gated by a recall/edge-probe
  harness ("verifier reach = spec reach").
- **Reliability (Phase 4):** "cleaner" = fewer confusing failure modes on the newcomer happy
  path.

The phrase is a direction, **not** a mandate to delete by usage count. The hard rules in
`STREAMS.md` and `BACKLOG-SCHEMA.md` keep "tighter" from becoming "broke a load-bearing
instruction the week the spotlight landed."
